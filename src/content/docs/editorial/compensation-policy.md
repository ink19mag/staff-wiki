_Effective May 2026_

_Ink 19_ is an all-volunteer publication. Our credibility with readers, artists, and the industry depends on the fact that nothing we publish is for sale. This policy describes how that principle works in practice for staff and contributors.

## Summary

- _Ink 19_ does not pay staff or contributors for their work.
- Staff may accept review materials and event access required to do the work.
- Customary press hospitality at an event (a meal, drinks, a swag bag offered to all attending press) is acceptable; gifts or favors directed at you specifically are not.
- Staff may not accept payment, gifts, or other consideration from artists, publicists, labels, studios, venues, or other industry parties in connection with _Ink 19_ coverage.
- When in doubt, ask the editors before accepting.

## § 1 — Volunteer Status

_Ink 19_ does not provide financial compensation to its staff or contributors. We are volunteers because that's how we keep editorial decisions independent of commercial pressure. Writing for _Ink 19_ is not a paid position, and contributors should not represent it as one to publicists, artists, or any other party.

## § 2 — What You May Accept

In the course of doing the work, contributors may accept the following from artists, labels, publicists, studios, venues, and PR firms:

- **Review materials.** Streaming links, download codes, physical media, books, screeners, and gear sent for review. Digital and physical materials are yours to keep, unless the publicist states otherwise, and are subject to the [restrictions in the Staff Guide](staff-guide#how-we-work) on sharing private streams and downloads.
- **Event access.** Press credentials, photo passes, comp tickets, and a plus-one where standard for press.
- **Customary press hospitality.** Food, drinks, and swag bags offered to all attending press at an event.

## § 3 — What You May Not Accept

In connection with _Ink 19_ coverage, contributors may not accept any of the following from artists, publicists, labels, studios, venues, or other industry parties:

- **Cash, gift cards, or cash-equivalent compensation** of any kind.
- **Gifts directed at you specifically** that go beyond what's offered to the press pool: meals out, hotel stays, paid hospitality, branded items of meaningful value, personal favors.
- **Payment to write, place, slant, or alter coverage.** This includes "paid placements," sponsored reviews, and any arrangement where coverage is contingent on consideration. _Ink 19_ does not run paid editorial.
- **Trades** — agreeing to favorable coverage in exchange for materials, access, or any other benefit.

If you receive something in this category unsolicited, [tell an editor](mailto:editors@ink19.com). Most of the time the answer will be to politely return it or pass it along; the important thing is that we know about it.

## § 4 — Outside Work

This policy applies to your work for _Ink 19_. Contributors are free to take paid work from labels, artists, publicists, or anyone else outside of _Ink 19_.

What you can't do is cover the same subject for _Ink 19_. If you're being paid by a label, work in PR for an artist, manage a band, or have any other compensated relationship with an industry party, you cannot review, interview, or otherwise cover that party (or their clients/roster) under your _Ink 19_ byline. Tell the editors about the relationship so we can route the coverage to someone else.

## § 5 — Disclosure & When to Ask

Use the editors as a resource. If you're not sure whether something is acceptable — an unusual gift, an after-party invite, an offer that doesn't fit neatly into the categories above — [ask before accepting](mailto:editors@ink19.com). It is always easier to clear something in advance than to explain it after the fact.

## § 6 — Consequences of Violation

Failure to observe this policy may result in:

- Removal of affected coverage from _Ink 19_ platforms.
- A published correction or editor's note.
- Suspension of publishing privileges pending review.
- **Dismissal from _Ink 19_ staff** in cases of paid placement, undisclosed industry relationships, or any arrangement that compromises editorial independence.

A single instance of accepting payment to publish, slant, or kill a story is grounds for immediate dismissal at editorial discretion, without prior warning.

## § 7 — Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed no less than annually, or sooner if circumstances require. Questions should be directed to the publisher.
