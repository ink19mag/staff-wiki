*Ink 19* exists because our writers have something original to say about the music, media, and thought they are experiencing firsthand. We write to contribute original ideas to the world, not to please publicists, artists, managers, or anyone. To this end, *Ink 19* does not condone plagiarism in any form.

Plagiarism is the act of presenting other writers’ work as your own. With the exception of facts (contact details, release dates, tour information, etc.) **copying** phrases, thoughts, and commentary directly from one or more sources, including press releases, articles, the band’s website, etc., without disclosing who wrote them is plagiarism. **Rephrasing** existing reviews from press releases or other sources without disclosing who wrote them is plagiarism. **Borrowing** other writers’ ideas and thoughts without telling the reader that you did not come up with them yourself is plagiarism. Don’t do it.

This is not to say that other writers’ words must be ignored. If a phrase exists that perfectly supports the original ideas in your review, then include it — along with some information about the person who wrote that phrase and where you found it.

*Ink 19* takes all accusations of plagiarism seriously, and we diligently investigate every suspected incident. Writers determined to have plagiarized a review will be removed from staff, and their articles will be permanently removed from the *Ink 19* site.

We understand that false accusations are sometimes made. In this case, the originality of your work and the citations you include in it go a long way toward helping us defend your writing.

Please remember why it is that you write, and only submit 100% original work to *Ink 19*. We don’t care to read the same ideas that are already published elsewhere on the web — we want to know what *you* really think.
