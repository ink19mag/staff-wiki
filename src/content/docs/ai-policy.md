_Effective April 2026_

_Ink 19_ is committed to honest, original, and human music and culture journalism. This policy governs how artificial intelligence tools — including large language models, image generators, and related technologies — may and may not be used by _Ink 19_ staff and contributors. These rules exist to protect our writers, readers, sources, and the integrity of the publication. We believe in human voices writing about human music. AI is a tool, not a byline.

## Summary

- The author is always responsible for the accuracy and originality of the final work.
- Authors must personally verify any AI output for hallucinations, plagiarism, and bad citations.
- AI tools must be disclosed to editors, and are permitted only as defined in this policy.
- AI-generated images, quotations, and wholesale articles are not allowed.
- All quotations, background material, and images must come from real, verifiable sources.
- Confidential source material and interviews must never be fed into third-party AI tools.

## § 1 — Permitted Use

- Research assistance, background gathering, fact-checking.
- Spell-check, grammar, outlining, structural suggestions.
- Translation assistance.
- Transcription clean-up.
- Headline and caption brainstorming.

## § 2 — Prohibited Use

- **No AI-generated images, audio or video**.
- **No AI-fabricated quotations.** Direct quotations must come from firsthand interviews, documented press materials, or verifiable published sources.
- **No AI-written articles**. Any content generated wholesale by AI, rather than assisted by AI, is not acceptable under any byline. If you did not write any part of a sentence, do not use it.
- **No AI-summarized proprietary content**. Paywalled, embargoed, or proprietary content must be accessed via legitimate logins and used according to relevant copyright policies.

## § 3 — Disclosure Requirements

- **Authors must disclose use of AI tools upon submission**: undisclosed AI use discovered post-publication is a violation of this policy.
- **Spell-check, minor grammar suggestions**: a brief memo in the article’s comments suffices. No reader-facing disclosure is required.
- **AI-assisted translation, structural outlining, transcription**: disclosure of the tool and its contribution is required in the article’s comments and at the foot of the article itself: *"This article was drafted with the assistance of [tool]. All content was reviewed and verified by the author.”*

## § 4 — Author Responsibility & Verification

The author bears full editorial and ethical responsibility for any AI-assisted content appearing under their byline. This responsibility is non-transferable. Specifically:

- **Hallucinations.** AI tools are known to generate plausible-sounding but false statements, including fabricated album titles, fictitious chart positions, invented biographical details, and nonexistent quotes. Authors must independently verify all factual claims produced with AI assistance before submission.
- **Plagiarism.** AI tools often reproduce or closely paraphrase copyrighted material without attribution. Authors are responsible for ensuring that AI-assisted passages are accurate and do not infringe on third-party intellectual property. When in doubt, rewrite from scratch.
- **Accuracy of sourcing.** AI-generated citations, links, or references must be checked against the original sources. Authors may not cite any source they have not themselves consulted.
- **Voice and editorial judgment.** Critical assessments, interpretations, and opinions published under a byline must represent the author's genuine views. Authors should not publish AI-generated opinions as their own without exercising independent critical judgment.

## § 5 — Editorial Oversight

- Editors reserve the right to request a description of how AI tools were used in the production of any submitted piece.
- Editors may require revisions or additional verification before publication where AI-assisted content raises factual or ethical concerns.
- Editors may conduct audits of published content for compliance with this policy.
- Corrections arising from AI hallucinations or undisclosed AI use will be published under _Ink 19_'s standard corrections policy and attributed accordingly.

## § 6 — Privacy & Source Protection

- Interview transcripts including off-the-record material, unpublished correspondence, and any information provided in confidence must not be submitted to third-party AI tools or cloud-based LLMs.
- Contributors should assume that any text entered into an external AI tool may be retained, logged, or used for model training unless the platform's terms explicitly state otherwise.

## § 7 — Consequences of Violation

Failure to observe any provision of this policy may result in:

- Immediate removal of the offending content from _Ink 19_ platforms.
- A published correction or editor's note at the discretion of the editors.
- Suspension of publishing privileges pending review.
- **Dismissal from _Ink 19_ staff** in cases of deliberate deception, repeated violations, or material harm to the publication's reputation or legal standing.

**Note:** These consequences are not graduated in all cases. A single instance of publishing fabricated quotes, submitting undisclosed AI-written copy as one's own work, or violating source confidentiality may be grounds for immediate dismissal at editorial discretion, without prior warning.

## § 8 — Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed no less than annually, or sooner in response to material changes in AI technology, industry standards, or applicable law. Contributors are responsible for remaining current with the latest version as published on the _Ink 19_ staff site. Questions should be directed to the publisher.
